About

ATL Marathon

The ATL Marathon is Atal Innovation Mission’s flagship innovation challenge, where schools identify community problems of their choice and develop innovative solutions in the form of working prototypes. The last edition of the Marathon witnessed 12000+ innovations, the top 400 of them got internship opportunities through the Student Innovator Program with leading corporates and Incubation Centres of India, prizes and certificates from AIM, NITI Aayog.


In ATL Marathon 2023-24, we want the students of India to choose any of the below problem statements or work on a problem that they find around themselves, research, ideate, innovate and implement a solution for it.

Look at few exciting projects from our Student Innovators

Cartoonify.NFTs

In the midst of a journey to Bangalore, they met remarkable painters and their vibrant creations. Their curiosity led to uncovering a disheartening truth: these skilled painters earned a meager monthly income of just 5000 rupees. This revelation became the driving force behind their project, Cartoonify.NFTs, earning them an invitation to a 6-week internship program In the same year, they earned recognition as one of the top 100 projects of ATL marathon 2021.
-Omkar Kumbhar, Dev Kore, and Param Shah , Karnataka

Glucobet

A group of friends discovered themselves motivated by a common goal - to lessen the hardships of a loved one enduring the everyday rigors of severe diabetes. And thus, Glucobelt was born - a device as ingenious as it was compassionate. What set their creation apart was its ability to predict sugar levels in food before consumption and in the blood without the need for painful needle pricks.

-Owee Prasad Adawadkar, Sanavi Chikhale, Mihika Tushar Datar

Replastic

The trio understood the urgent need for a solution that would put an end to this plastic epidemic giving birth to REPLASTIC. Idea was that people could schedule plastic pickups from the comfort of their homes or offices through RePlastic's website. On the chosen date and time, a team of dedicated rag pickers, employed by the organization, would arrive to collect the plastic.They participated in the ATL (Atal Tinkering Labs) Marathon, a platform that celebrated innovation and problem-solving.
-Gunika Beriwal, Abhinav Tejpaul, and Satyam Verma
